How to Cook Beans

Note: canned beans are cheap, and will work for most of these recipes (not the soups). Canned beans tend to be mushy, and have too much liquid — pour it off and reserve it in case you want to add it back.

To cook dried beans, first go over them very carefully to remove rocks and mudballs, which can ruin an entire meal. Pouring the beans into a white pan or baking sheet, a small amount at a time, works well.

Soak the beans overnight in a large amount of water. Split peas need only a few hours, and lentils only an hour.

Drain the old water and just cover the beans with fresh water (at least twice as much water for soups).

Bring the pot to a boil, then turn down heat, cover, and simmer 1-3 hours until tender but not mushy. Stir occasionally. Cooking time varies because of bean size and age. Lentils cook in about 30 minutes, split peas in 1-1 ½ hours. Soups can be overcooked, as you generally want the beans to be soft.
